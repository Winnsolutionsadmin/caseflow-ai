# CaseFlow AI Ideal Customer Profile & Prospect Criteria

## Primary Target Segment

### Firm Size & Structure:
- **Attorneys:** 1-10 attorneys
- **Staff:** 2-15 total employees (attorneys + paralegals + admin)
- **Annual Revenue:** $500K - $3M
- **Cases per Month:** 15-75 active cases
- **Geographic Focus:** Sacramento, Placer, El Dorado Counties (Phase 1)

### Practice Areas Most Likely to Buy:

#### Tier 1 (Highest Probability):
**Personal Injury Law**
- High deadline sensitivity (statute of limitations critical)
- Volume-driven practice (20-50+ cases simultaneously)
- Client communication intensive
- Revenue per case: $15K-50K average
- Key Pain Points: Missed deadlines = malpractice, client complaints about updates

**Family Law**
- Court date heavy (multiple appearances per case)
- Emotional client base requiring frequent communication
- Document intensive (custody agreements, financial disclosures)
- Revenue per case: $5K-25K average
- Key Pain Points: Missed court dates, client anxiety, billing disputes

#### Tier 2 (Good Probability):
**Criminal Defense**
- Strict court deadlines and appearance requirements
- High client anxiety requiring regular updates
- Case volume varies but deadline-critical
- Revenue per case: $2K-15K average
- Key Pain Points: Court calendar conflicts, client communication, case tracking

**Immigration Law**
- Federal deadline complexity (USCIS, DOL, various agencies)
- High client stress and communication needs
- Document assembly intensive
- Revenue per case: $1K-8K average
- Key Pain Points: Federal deadline tracking, multilingual client communication

#### Tier 3 (Moderate Probability):
**Estate Planning**
- Document generation heavy
- Client relationship focused
- Less deadline intensive but still present
- Revenue per case: $1K-5K average
- Key Pain Points: Document assembly, client onboarding, follow-up

## Technology Indicators (High-Value Signals)

### Positive Indicators (Ready to Buy):
- **Still using paper file systems** (60%+ of case files in physical form)
- **Outlook calendar for all deadline tracking** (no specialized legal software)
- **Manual conflict checking** (attorney reviews each case manually)
- **Email-only client communication** (no client portal)
- **Separate billing software** (QuickBooks + time tracking spreadsheets)
- **Paralegal overwhelmed with admin tasks** (30+ hours/week on non-legal work)
- **Recent growth but same systems** (doubled cases but no tech upgrades)

### Negative Indicators (Not Ready/Poor Fit):
- Recently invested in major practice management system (<12 months ago)
- Full-time IT staff or dedicated tech person
- Highly customized existing workflows
- Primarily transactional practice (real estate closings, contracts)
- Extremely price-sensitive (competing primarily on low fees)

**Criteria:**
- Title: "Managing Partner," "Senior Partner," "Founding Partner"
- Company Size: 2-50 employees
- Industry: Law Practice
- Location: Sacramento, CA (25-mile radius)
- Keywords: "personal injury," "family law," "criminal defense," "immigration"

`(title:"Managing Partner" OR title:"Senior Partner" OR title:"Founding Partner") AND (company:"Law" OR company:"Legal" OR company:"Attorney") AND (location:"Sacramento" OR location:"Roseville" OR location:"Folsom")`

- Company Size: 2-50 employees
- Industry: Law Practice  
- Location: Sacramento, CA (25-mile radius)

### Yelp & Avvo Prospecting:

#### Yelp Filters:
- Category: Lawyers, Personal Injury Law, Family Law, Criminal Defense
- Location: Sacramento, Roseville, Folsom, Placerville
- Rating: 3.5+ stars (shows client interaction, good or challenging)
- Review Volume: 10+ reviews (indicates active practice)

#### Qualification from Reviews:
- **High Review Volume:** Indicates busy practice with client communication challenges
- **Communication Complaints:** "Hard to reach," "Don't return calls," "Never knew what was happening"
- **Positive Reviews Mentioning Staff:** Suggests team size of 3+ people

#### Avvo Lawyer Directory:
- **Advanced Search Filters:**
  - Practice Areas: Personal Injury, Family, Criminal, Immigration
  - Years Practicing: 5-25 years (established but not set in ways)
  - Client Reviews: Available (indicates client-facing practice)
  - Peer Reviews: 4.0+ (respected in legal community)

**Red Flags on Avvo:**
- Solo practice with 25+ years (may be set in traditional ways)
- No client reviews (potentially low-volume practice)
- Single practice area focus unless it's high-volume (estate planning only)

## Sacramento County Bar Association Approach

### Member Directory Strategy:
**Access Methods:**
- Purchase member directory (if available for marketing)
- Attend networking events for direct relationship building
- Sponsor CLE events for targeted exposure
- Partner with bar committees (Technology, Small Firm)

**Target Member Segments:**
- **Small Firm Section members** (self-identified as small practice)
- **Young Lawyers Section** (3-10 years experience, growth-minded)
- **Family Law Section** (high client communication needs)
- **Criminal Law Section** (deadline-critical practice)

### Bar Association Event Strategy:
**Monthly Networking Events:**
- Attend regularly to build relationships
- Focus on office managers and practice administrators
- Collect business cards with notes about pain points
- Follow up within 48 hours with relevant resources

**Annual Events:**
- **Bar Awards Dinner** - Sponsor table, invite prospects
- **Annual Meeting** - Booth/exhibit space
- **Golf Tournament** - Networking opportunity with decision-makers

## Regional Expansion Criteria

### Placer County (Phase 1 Expansion):
**Target Cities:** Roseville, Auburn, Lincoln, Rocklin
**Key Indicators:**
- Rapid population growth = increased legal needs
- Higher average income = willingness to pay for technology
- Commuter community = attorneys may work non-traditional hours
- Growing business community = increased legal services demand

**Search Focus:**
- Attorneys serving both Sacramento and Placer counties
- Family law practices (high divorce rates in growing communities)
- Personal injury (increased traffic/accidents with growth)
- Real estate/business law (commercial growth)

### El Dorado County (Phase 2 Expansion):
**Target Cities:** Placerville, South Lake Tahoe, Cameron Park
**Key Indicators:**  
- Tourism-driven economy = seasonal legal needs
- Rural practice challenges = efficiency needs high
- Limited local competition = easier market entry
- High-net-worth clients in Tahoe area = price tolerance

**Search Focus:**
- General practice attorneys (rural areas require versatility)
- Personal injury (outdoor recreation accidents)
- Real estate (vacation property transactions)
- Criminal defense (DUI common in tourism areas)

## Prospecting Workflow

### Week 1: Research & List Building
**Monday-Tuesday:** LinkedIn Sales Navigator searches (50 prospects)
**Wednesday-Thursday:** Google Maps/Yelp research (25 prospects)  
**Friday:** Avvo directory research (25 prospects)
**Target:** 100 qualified prospects per week

### Week 2-3: Outreach Sequence
**Email Sequence:** 5 emails over 2 weeks (see OUTREACH.md)
**Phone Calls:** After email 2 and email 4
**SMS Follow-up:** After emails 1, 3, and 5
**LinkedIn Messages:** After email 3

### Week 4: Follow-up & Qualification
**Demo Requests:** Schedule and conduct
**No Response:** Move to nurture sequence
**Not Interested:** Document reason, add to future campaign (6 months)
**Qualified Opportunities:** Move to sales process

## Disqualification Criteria

### Firm Characteristics:
- **Too Small:** Solo attorney with no staff (unless high-volume)
- **Too Large:** 10+ attorneys (Enterprise sales process required)
- **Wrong Practice Areas:** Primarily transactional (real estate closings, corporate contracts)
- **Geographic:** Outside target counties (Phase 1)

### Technology Situation:
- **Recent Major Investment:** New practice management system <6 months ago
- **Highly Technical:** In-house IT staff, custom-built systems
- **Integration Complexity:** 10+ existing software tools that must integrate

### Financial Indicators:
- **Price Shopping Only:** "What's your cheapest option?"
- **No ROI Interest:** Won't discuss efficiency improvements or revenue impact
- **Cash Flow Issues:** Multiple mentions of budget constraints, delayed decision-making

### Behavioral Red Flags:
- **Disrespectful to Staff:** Rude to receptionist/paralegal (indicates poor culture fit)
- **Unrealistic Expectations:** Expects 100% customization without Enterprise pricing
- **Analysis Paralysis:** Requests extensive documentation but never moves forward

## Ideal Prospect Profile Summary

**The Perfect CaseFlow Customer:**
- 3-5 attorney personal injury or family law firm
- 5+ year practice, established reputation
- 25-50 active cases at any time
- Currently using Outlook + QuickBooks + paper files
- Office manager struggling with client communication
- Recent growth but same manual systems
- Sacramento/Placer County location
- Annual revenue $800K-2M
- Professional but pragmatic decision-making process
- Willing to invest in efficiency for measurable ROI

**Key Qualifying Questions:**
1. "How many active cases do you typically manage at once?"
2. "What's your current system for tracking court deadlines?"
3. "How often do clients ask for case status updates?"
4. "What's the biggest operational challenge in your practice right now?"
5. "How much time do attorneys spend on administrative tasks weekly?"
